The **Análisis Instrumental** lab (`analisis-instrumental.php`) is a step up from the General Chemistry lab. It focuses on the specialised, high-precision equipment used to identify and quantify the chemical composition of samples. The lab description in the source states the key distinction plainly:

> _"A diferencia del laboratorio de química general, que se centra en reacciones y experimentos básicos, en un laboratorio de análisis instrumental se emplean equipos de alta precisión para identificar y cuantificar sustancias en muestras de alimentos, medicamentos, materiales, suelos, aguas, entre otros."_

In practice this means you will find larger, more complex instruments here — each with a purpose-built 3D model and a contextual description visible from the card info panel.

## How these instruments differ from general chemistry equipment

The General Chemistry lab covers hand tools and basic glassware — things you pick up and manipulate directly. The instruments here share three distinguishing characteristics:

1. **Precision** — Equipment such as the centrifugadora and placa calefactora operates under controlled parameters (speed, temperature) rather than manual technique.
2. **Specialised purpose** — Each instrument performs a narrow, well-defined analytical or preparation function. The autoclave, for example, is used solely for sterilisation; the campana de extracción is a safety system, not a reaction vessel.
3. **Sample preparation focus** — Several instruments (centrifugadora, incubadora, autoclave) are used to prepare or condition samples before analysis, rather than to run experiments directly.

## Interacting with instrument cards

<Steps>
  <Step title="Load the lab">
    Navigate to `analisis-instrumental.php`. The instrument grid loads with all six cards visible below the hero section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inspect the 3D model">
    Each card auto-rotates its model on load. Click and drag to rotate manually; scroll or pinch to zoom in and out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the instrument description">
    Click the **i** button (top-right corner of the card) to flip to the text panel. Each instrument has a one-sentence description explaining its function.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the 3D model">
    Click the **i** button again to switch back to the model view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the AR experience">
    Click **Ver en Realidad Aumentada** to open the AR viewer. On a supported mobile device the model will appear overlaid on your camera feed. On desktop, a QR code image opens instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>
