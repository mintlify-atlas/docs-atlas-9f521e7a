The **Química General** lab (`quimica-general.php`) is designed for introductory chemistry. It provides 3D models of the hand tools, glassware, and accessories you would encounter in a foundational chemistry course. The goal is to let students familiarise themselves with each instrument's appearance, name, and purpose before or after handling the real object.

## Instruments

The lab contains nine instrument cards arranged in a 3-column grid. Each instrument has a fully interactive 3D model and an AR experience.

### Instrument descriptions

- **Gradilla** — A rack used to hold multiple test tubes upright during experiments.
- **Matraz** — An Erlenmeyer flask; conical glassware used for mixing, heating, and storing solutions.
- **Microscopio** — An optical microscope for magnifying samples too small to see with the naked eye.
- **Mortero con mano de porcelana** — A porcelain mortar and pestle used to grind and crush solid substances into a fine powder.
- **Pipeta** — A graduated glass tube used to measure and transfer precise volumes of liquid.
- **Placas de porcelana para reacciones** — Porcelain spot plates with multiple wells for performing small-scale chemical reactions and color tests.
- **Pinzas para matraces** — Metal clamps designed to hold Erlenmeyer flasks and round-bottom flasks securely, especially during heating.
- **Anillo de hierro** — An iron ring attached to a support stand, used to hold funnels, crucibles, or glassware at a fixed height.
- **Pinzas para tubo de ensayo** — Wooden or metal tongs for gripping test tubes safely, particularly when they are hot.

## Interacting with instrument cards

<Steps>
  <Step title="Load the lab">
    Navigate to `quimica-general.php`. The page loads with the instrument grid visible below the hero banner. Each card shows its 3D model auto-rotating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rotate and inspect the 3D model">
    Click and drag (or touch-drag on mobile) inside a card to rotate the model. Use scroll or pinch to zoom. The model viewer is powered by Google's `<model-viewer>` component.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View the instrument description">
    Click the circular **i** button in the top-right corner of any card. The 3D model view slides away and a text panel appears with the instrument's name and description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the 3D model">
    Click the **i** button again to toggle back to the model view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the AR experience">
    Click **Ver en Realidad Aumentada** at the bottom of any card. On a compatible mobile device this launches the AR viewer directly. On desktop it may open a QR code image that you can scan with your phone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## AR capability

All nine instruments have the `ar` attribute set on their `<model-viewer>` elements, meaning the AR viewer is enabled for every card. However, the AR link targets for **Pipeta**, **Pinzas para matraces**, **Anillo de hierro**, and **Pinzas para tubo de ensayo** are currently set to `//` (a placeholder), so those buttons do not yet open a functional AR scene.

The following instruments have working AR links pointing to external mywebar.com or local QR image targets:

| Instrument | AR target type |
|---|---|
| Gradilla | External (mywebar.com) |
| Matraz | External (mywebar.com) |
| Microscopio | External (mywebar.com) |
| Mortero con mano de porcelana | External (mywebar.com) |
| Placas de porcelana para reacciones | Local QR image (`img/QR/quimicaGen/matraz.jpg`) |
