The **Plantas Químicas** lab (`plantas-quimicas.php`) covers industrial-scale chemical processes — the kind used in real manufacturing plants rather than benchtop experiments. The source describes its purpose as:

> _"Un laboratorio de plantas químicas es un espacio especializado donde se realizan estudios, pruebas y simulaciones de los procesos químicos utilizados en la industria. Su objetivo principal es diseñar, optimizar y controlar la producción de sustancias químicas a gran escala, garantizando seguridad, eficiencia y calidad en los procesos industriales."_

## Industries represented

The lab explicitly covers the following industrial sectors:

- Petrochemical (fuels, plastics, hydrocarbons)
- Pharmaceutical (drug synthesis and purification)
- Food processing (quality control, contaminant detection)
- Plastics and materials manufacturing
- Energy (biofuels, renewable energy processes)

## Current content

|---|---|
| Objectives | Design and optimise industrial processes; evaluate viability; reduce costs and environmental impact; run simulations. |
| Main lab areas | Reaction area, separation/purification zone (distillers, filters), quality control area, safety and storage zone. |
| Equipment and techniques | Chemical reactors, distillers and evaporators, heat exchangers, chromatography, spectroscopy, computational simulation. |
| Processes studied | Fuels and petrochemicals, drug synthesis, water and waste treatment, fertiliser production, biofuels and renewables. |
| Safety rules | PPE use, handling of flammables and toxics, monitoring temperature and pressure, emergency protocols, waste minimisation. |
| Conclusion | Summary of the lab's role in chemical industry development. |
