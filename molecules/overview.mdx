## What are molecular structures?

The **Molecular Structures** section of RALQ provides interactive 3D models of chemical compounds. Each model lets you examine how atoms are arranged in space, how chemical bonds connect them, and what the overall geometry of the molecule looks like.

Understanding molecular structure is foundational to chemistry. The three-dimensional shape of a molecule determines its physical properties, reactivity, polarity, and biological behavior. RALQ makes this tangible by letting you rotate, zoom, and place molecules in your physical environment through augmented reality.

<Note>
RALQ's molecular models are designed for educational use at the introductory and intermediate chemistry level. They are suitable for high school and university students studying organic chemistry, inorganic chemistry, and related disciplines.
</Note>

## How to access molecular structures

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to RALQ">
    Sign in with your account credentials at the RALQ login page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the main menu">
    After logging in, you will be taken to the main menu (`menu.php`). This page lists all sections of the platform.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Estructuras Moleculares">
    Select the **Estructuras Moleculares** option from the menu. This takes you to the molecular structures page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a molecule">
    You will see a grid of molecule cards. Each card displays a live 3D preview of the molecule. Click or tap any card to start interacting with it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you will find

The molecular structures page presents a **grid of molecule cards**. Each card contains:

- A live 3D model viewer that auto-rotates the molecule
- The molecule's name and chemical formula
- A blue **i** button to expand a description panel with formula, physical state, and molecular weight
- An **AR button** to launch the molecule in augmented reality on a mobile device

The catalog currently includes organic molecules such as caffeine, phenol, glycerol, and benzyl ethanol; an inorganic molecule (water); a simple hydrocarbon (propane); and several lab instruments. See the [full catalog](/molecules/catalog) for details.

## Interaction modes

RALQ supports two ways to explore molecular models:

**Browser 3D viewer** — available on any device with a modern browser. You can rotate, zoom, and inspect the model directly on screen. This mode works on desktop and mobile.

**Mobile augmented reality** — available on supported iOS and Android devices. Tap the AR button on a molecule card to place the model on a real-world surface using your phone's camera. You can walk around the molecule and view it at true scale in your physical environment.
